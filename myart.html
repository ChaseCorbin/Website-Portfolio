  • Pen.

    When most people draw they use a pencil, I decided to try a new medium that requires alot more control of decision making. It adds alot to the natural aspect when you allow mistakes to happen.

  • Shadows

    I found this reference very intriging as it expresses the different ways light can be illustrated. The water bends the light. The lemon blocks some light that passes through the middle (pulp and pitch) of the fruit changing its value and intensity. The front face you are viewing is not dark because of reflecting light from behind the perspective.

  • Translucent subjects have a shadow.

    I am trying to express the idea that light does not pass through glass perfectly, some reflects back, causing a shadow to cast. This is due to lack of photons reflecting from the surface behind the glass. It was an interesting physics inspired study of light. I grew a large interest as I studied the double slit experirement.

  • Embossing thick paper.

    I learned after many years that embossing thick heavy-weighted paper that is smooth can create extreme highlights. This can be controlled if done correctly without destroying your paper. The idea is to score the paper in a way to allow the white paper underneath to not be drawn over. This allows perfect highlights for fir textures in some cases.
